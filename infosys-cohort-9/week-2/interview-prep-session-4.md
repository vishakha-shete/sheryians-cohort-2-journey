# 📄 Pragati: Path to Future – Cohort 9

## Week 2 | Session 2 – Interview Readiness

## 📌 Overview

Today's session focused on **Interview Readiness**, helping participants understand that interviews are not about memorizing answers or reading a resume—they are about confidently communicating your story, skills, and impact.

The session covered how to develop the right mindset, structure self-introductions, present achievements effectively, and transform interview anxiety into confidence.

---

# 🎯 Key Learnings

## 1. Tell Me About Yourself

A self-introduction should **not** be a summary of your resume.

Instead, it should be:

* Brief
* Logical
* Relevant to the role
* Conversational
* Focused on your journey

An interview is a conversation—not a speech.

---

## 2. The 5 Pillars of a Successful Interview

### 🧠 Mindset

Develop confidence before entering the interview.

### 📄 Resume

Your resume should support your story, not replace it.

### 📖 Story

Every answer should have a beginning, middle, and end.

### 🎯 Presence

Be fully present during the interview.
Listen carefully before answering.

### 🤝 Close

End the interview professionally by thanking the interviewer and expressing enthusiasm.

---

# 🌱 Fixed Mindset vs Growth Mindset

## ❌ Fixed Mindset

* I'm not good at interviews.
* I'll probably fail.
* They're trying to reject me.
* I avoid feedback.
* Skills are something people are born with.

---

## ✅ Growth Mindset

* Interviewing is a skill that can be developed.
* Every interview teaches something new.
* Feedback helps me improve.
* The interviewer wants me to succeed too.

# 💡 Reframing Nervousness

According to research by **Harvard Professor Alison Wood Brooks**, changing the way we describe our emotions can improve interview performance.

Instead of saying:

❌ "I'm nervous."

Say:

✅ "I'm excited."

This simple mindset shift changes your perspective before entering the interview.

# 📄 Resume Evidence Bank

Instead of memorizing your resume, build an **Evidence Bank**.

Remember the **PPP Framework**

## Profile

Who are you?

Example:

> Computer Science Engineering graduate with knowledge of JavaScript, React, Node.js and MongoDB.

---

## Position

Which role are you applying for?

Example:

> Frontend Developer

or

> Full Stack Developer

---

## Proof

Support your profile with evidence.

Examples:

* Built multiple MERN Stack projects
* Completed internship/training
* Solved DSA problems
* Participated in hackathons
* Completed industry certifications

---

# 📈 Upgrade Resume Using XYZ Formula

Instead of writing duties...

Write achievements.

## Formula

**Achieved X**

Measured by **Y**

By doing **Z**

Example:

❌ Responsible for managing social media.

✅ Increased Instagram reach by **40%** by launching a weekly Reels content strategy.

---

Another Example

❌ Worked on a project.

✅ Built a full-stack movie discovery platform using React, Node.js, Express, and MongoDB, implementing JWT authentication and TMDB API integration.

# 🎤 Interview Tips

* Treat interviews as conversations.
* Don't memorize scripts.
* Share stories instead of facts.
* Quantify your achievements.
* Be present during the discussion.
* Listen before answering.
* Confidence comes from preparation and practice.

---

# ⭐ My Biggest Takeaways

* Interviews are conversations, not interrogations.
* Confidence is built through preparation.
* Storytelling makes answers memorable.
* Evidence is stronger than claims.
* Replace nervousness with excitement.
* Focus on impact, not responsibilities.
* Every interview is an opportunity to learn and improve.
